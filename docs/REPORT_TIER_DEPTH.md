# NICO report tier depth

NICO produces a separate evidence-bound report for each assessment tier. More detailed does not mean more permissive: every tier preserves explicit authorization, unavailable evidence, exact-run identity where applicable, human review, and blocked client delivery until approval.

## Express — detail level 1

Express is the concise technical-health baseline. It prioritizes a fast executive summary, section scorecard, evidence and findings, unavailable-data disclosures, quick wins, and a bounded improvement plan.

## Mid — detail level 2

Mid includes the Express baseline and adds:

- snapshot-bound evidence identity;
- explicit evidence-coverage accounting;
- review-by-exception detail;
- integrity hashes and report bindings;
- an executive quick view derived from retained section truth;
- categorized evidence counts;
- decision-support summaries of verified areas and areas awaiting verification; and
- a clear statement of what Full adds next.

The Mid executive layer is derived only from existing Mid evidence. It does not change scores, upgrade evidence, approve findings, or permit client delivery.

## Full — detail level 3

Full is the deepest assessment report. It includes the Mid evidence and review layers plus:

- cross-domain synthesis across retained assessment sections;
- a bounded risk register derived from existing findings;
- expanded remediation guidance;
- verification steps and rollback requirements;
- final-review preparation; and
- the broadest supported evidence appendix.

Full depth is attached before Markdown, HTML, JSON, and PDF rendering. Existing section scores and section truth remain unchanged. The added executive summary and action plan are derived from retained Full evidence and remain advisory.

Full remains evidence-bound and human-review-required. A more detailed report is not an automatic approval, certification, vulnerability-free claim, or permission to make production changes.

## Product rule

Each tier must produce its own report artifact:

1. Express report: concise baseline.
2. Mid report: Express baseline plus deeper evidence and decision support.
3. Full report: Mid depth plus the broadest supported analysis, remediation, verification, and review preparation.

Missing or failed evidence remains unavailable or blocked. It is never filled with placeholders merely to make a higher tier appear more complete.
