# NICO Commercial Licensing Workflow

This guide explains how to move from interest in NICO to a signed commercial license without confusing evaluation rights, commercial rights, and delivery approval.

This is an operational checklist, not legal advice. Review commercial documents with qualified counsel before using them with customers, clients, contractors, enterprise buyers, or government agencies.

## Files involved

- `LICENSE`: default source-available non-commercial license.
- `COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md`: commercial license agreement template.
- `docs/commercial-license-order-form.md`: order form template for defining the exact customer scope.

## When a commercial license is required

A commercial license is required before any business, paid, client-facing, managed-service, hosted-service, contractor, government, or revenue-generating use.

The default license is for non-commercial evaluation, testing, education, and defensive research only.

## Standard workflow

1. Confirm the proposed use is defensive and authorized.
2. Confirm whether the buyer needs internal use, client-facing use, managed-service use, hosted-service use, or a custom commercial scope.
3. Fill out `docs/commercial-license-order-form.md` with the customer name, contacts, permitted use, users, repositories, environments, term, fees, support level, and special restrictions.
4. Attach or reference `COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md` as the governing commercial terms.
5. Add any negotiated special terms directly in the order form.
6. Have both sides sign or otherwise accept the order form.
7. Store the final signed order form and linked commercial license with the deal record.
8. Do not provide commercial access, client-facing rights, managed-service rights, hosted-service rights, or production use until the signed scope is complete.

## Scope checklist

Before approval, confirm the order form states:

- licensee legal name;
- billing and technical contacts;
- license type;
- permitted use;
- allowed users or seats;
- allowed repositories or systems;
- allowed deployment environments;
- client-facing rights yes or no;
- managed-service rights yes or no;
- hosted-service rights yes or no;
- resale or white-label rights yes or no;
- license term;
- fees and payment due date;
- support level;
- data or deployment restrictions;
- special terms; and
- acceptance signatures.

## Red flags before signing

Pause for review if the buyer requests:

- unlimited use without a defined fee;
- redistribution rights;
- white-label rights;
- sublicensing rights;
- broad government or contractor use;
- client-facing reports without a client-facing license;
- managed-service use without a managed-service license;
- hosted-service use without hosted-service rights;
- production decisions without human review; or
- removal of ownership, attribution, authorization, safety, or human-review language.

## Post-signing checklist

After signing:

- save the signed order form;
- save the agreed commercial license version;
- record the licensee, term, scope, renewal date, and payment terms;
- record any support commitment;
- record any custom restrictions;
- confirm access only matches the signed scope; and
- set a reminder before renewal or expiration.

## Guardrail

A signed commercial license grants only the rights stated in the signed scope. It does not approve unsafe use, remove authorization requirements, certify report accuracy, or replace human review.
