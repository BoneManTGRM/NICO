# Code of Conduct

## Standard

Participation in the NICO project requires professional, evidence-based, and respectful conduct.

Expected behavior includes:

- discussing technical claims with verifiable evidence;
- distinguishing confirmed facts, inferences, experiments, and opinions;
- respecting authorization, confidentiality, and security boundaries;
- reporting mistakes directly and correcting them without concealment;
- accepting review of safety, privacy, scoring, and deployment claims; and
- focusing criticism on the work rather than the person.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks;
- publishing private information or credentials;
- encouraging unauthorized access or offensive misuse;
- fabricating test, scanner, deployment, score, or client evidence;
- pressuring maintainers to bypass required checks or human-review gates; and
- using project spaces to distribute malware, phishing, exploit automation, or stolen data.

## Scope

This standard applies to repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, documentation, project communication, and public representation of NICO.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, reject, lock, or remove contributions that violate this standard. Serious security, privacy, or harassment incidents may result in immediate removal from project spaces.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through a non-public contact channel. Do not include credentials, private client data, or exploit details in a public report.

## Attribution

This is a project-specific conduct policy inspired by common open-source community standards. NICO is source-available under its repository license and is not represented as an open-source project when commercial-use restrictions apply.
